WORKFLOW

We will do everything possible to make it easy and transparent for the client to work with us, following a certain sequence. We want to show you a brief example of our workflow. It allows us to achieve the best results without exceeding project deadlines.

QUESTIONS

▼ What do we require from the client before beginning work?

We can work with almost any source data and materials. The client sends us what they consider necessary and important. We are very flexible and can quickly adapt to client requests. It is important to send a complete set of materials and descriptions. We need to collect all the information and materials before starting work. If something is not clear to us, we clarify the details with the client.

▼ Is it possible to add something to the workflow?

Our goal is for the client to be satisfied with our work. Therefore, we discuss any changes in advance. It is important to maintain communication in our work.

▼ What is included in the cost of our work?

The cost of our work includes all stages, from concept or sketch to final rendering and post-processing. Typically, our render size is at least 5000 pixels on the long side. The cost does not include file transfer, lifetime NDA, purchasing content from stock sites, or 3D modeling of additional objects that are not part of the project.

▼ What affects the cost?

Several factors can influence the cost of the entire project. The most common ones are:

- the complexity of the entire project;

- the deadlines for its completion; - the scale of the entire project;

- the level of detail; - the season in your project;

- the number and size of the final images.

These are all basic points. The best option would be to send us a request for a quote, and we will calculate everything according to your requirements and your technical specifications.

▼ How is payment made? Do you work on an advance payment basis?

We send the client an invoice that includes all the company's details, payment terms, and the amount. The terms of advance payment and payment stages are discussed individually based on the project's timeframe and size. Typically, we request a 50% advance payment or receive payment for completed project stages, especially for substantial projects. However, watermarks are usually added to images until the final payment ( or confirmation of payment) is received, after which we provide the original images without watermarks.

▼ What does project freezing involve?

There are cases when the client does not respond for a long time. We cannot continue the work and process. (The minimum term is 14 days) Then we freeze the project and require 50% of the total cost of the work if the client has received gray renders. If the renders are colored, we require full payment. However, this payment can be credited after the client's feedback.

▼ What changes in the project can lead to an increase in the cost of work?

Let's just say, we always strive to fit within the client's budget and try to avoid unforeseen costs. But there are cases that are not obvious at the initial stage and can lead to very significant changes in the final project.These can include:

- changes in the camera angle;

- additional angles;

- changes in design or its variations;

- changes in the season;

- changes in time (day or night).

▼ How to communicate with us?

Ideally, it is useful to have a single client representative who coordinates adjustments with us. Communication can take place via email or messenger. Additionally, we use the MIRO service where clients can leave comments on images, and we can receive clarifications on aspects that are unclear to our team. For file transfer, we use the DropBox service. Please note that we do not practice receiving feedback during phone calls.
